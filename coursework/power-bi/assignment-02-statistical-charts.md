# Assignment 02: Statistical Charts

This assignment focused on creating basic statistical visuals in Power BI using weather data.

## Dataset

The report used `seattle_weather.csv`, which contains daily weather records with fields such as date, precipitation, maximum temperature, minimum temperature, and rain status.

## Visuals Created

| Screenshot | Visualization | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `Assignment 2 Question 1.png` | Pie chart: Count of Date by Rain | Compares rainy and non-rainy days. The chart shows non-rainy days as the larger share and rainy days as the smaller share. |
| `Assignment 2 Question 2.png` | Scatter plot: TMAX and TMIN | Shows the relationship between maximum and minimum daily temperature. The plot shows a clear positive relationship between daily high and low temperatures. |
| `Assignment 2 Question 3.png` | Line chart: Average TMAX and Average TMIN by Month | Shows seasonal temperature patterns across the year. Both average highs and lows rise into summer and fall toward winter. |
| `Assignment 2 Question 4.png` | Bar chart: Count of Index by PRCP bins | Shows the distribution of precipitation amounts. Most records fall into the lowest precipitation bin. |
| `Assignment 2 Question 5.png` | Line chart: Sum of PRCP by Year | Shows yearly precipitation variation over time. |

## Skills Practiced

- Importing a CSV dataset into Power BI
- Creating pie, scatter, bar, and line charts
- Using date fields and date hierarchies
- Binning numerical data
- Comparing temperature and precipitation measures
- Reading distributions and trends from visualizations
